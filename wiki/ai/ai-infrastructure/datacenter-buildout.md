# The AI Datacenter Buildout

The largest infrastructure construction project in history is underway — driven
entirely by AI compute demand.

## Scale of Construction

- **23+ GW** of datacenter capacity under construction globally (end of September 2025)
- **~75%** of construction is in the United States
- **$700 billion** in datacenter projects planned for 2026 alone
- **$1 trillion+** committed by major tech companies across 2025-2026
- FY2027 spending expectations climbed **56%** between August 2025 and February 2026

## Notable Projects

- **Hyperion (Louisiana):** 2,250-acre site, $10B build cost, 5 GW capacity,
  powered by adjacent nuclear plant
- **Stargate (OpenAI + SoftBank + Oracle):** $500B AI infrastructure project
  announced January 2025
- Multiple GW-scale projects in Texas, Virginia, and the Midwest

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- Global AI capex reaches $1T in 2026 (matches real projections)
- OpenBrain capex: $200B; compute costs: $40B/year
- OpenBrain power requirement: 6 GW peak
- Global AI power: 38 GW peak
- China's CDZ at Tianwan: largest centralized cluster in the world

## The "Critical Infrastructure" Argument

The World Economic Forum (April 2026) argues AI infrastructure should be treated
as critical infrastructure — alongside power grids, water systems, and
telecommunications. The reasoning:
- AI increasingly underlies healthcare, finance, transportation, and defense
- Datacenter outages have cascading effects across the economy
- National security depends on compute access

## Key Takeaways

- AI infrastructure spending is growing 50%+ year-over-year with no plateau in sight
- The US dominates construction (~75% of capacity) reinforcing its compute advantage
- Individual projects are reaching GW-scale, rivaling power plants
- The AI 2027 scenario's infrastructure numbers are realistic, not exaggerated
- Power availability, not capital, is becoming the primary bottleneck
