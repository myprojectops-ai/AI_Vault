# AI's Energy Crisis

AI datacenters are consuming electricity at an unprecedented rate — and the
grid can't keep up.

## The Demand Problem

- AI is driving electricity demand to rise **1+ trillion kWh/year** through 2030
- Datacenters expected to account for **~20%** of that growth
- Annual datacenter consumption approaching the size of **Canada's entire power demand**
- Morgan Stanley forecasts US datacenter demand could reach **74 GW by 2028**
- Projected shortfall: **~49 GW** in available power access

## The Grid Bottleneck

- **72% of stakeholders** rate power and grid capacity as "very or extremely challenging"
- **7-year wait** on some connection requests to the grid
- The US electrical grid was stagnant for decades; AI is forcing unprecedented
  growth (from 1.34 TW total capacity)
- Legacy grid infrastructure wasn't designed for concentrated, high-density loads

## Power Solutions Being Pursued

### Nuclear
- Several datacenters colocating with nuclear plants (e.g., Hyperion in Louisiana)
- Microsoft signed a deal to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1
- SMRs (Small Modular Reactors) seen as long-term solution but years away

### Renewables + Storage
- Hyperscalers co-investing in renewable energy parks with generation and storage
- $20B+ investment in colocated solar/wind with batteries
- Google, Microsoft, Amazon all making massive renewable procurement deals

### On-Site Generation
- Natural gas turbines on-site for immediate power
- Fuel cells as backup and supplemental power
- Datacenters becoming grid stakeholders, not just consumers

### Demand Management
- Load flexibility: shifting compute to off-peak hours
- Efficiency improvements in AI chips (each generation more efficient per FLOP)
- Liquid cooling reducing power overhead

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- OpenBrain requires 6 GW peak power in 2026
- Global AI power: 38 GW peak in 2026
- China's CDZ at Tianwan nuclear plant: co-locating AI compute with nuclear power
- Power is explicitly identified as a bottleneck to compute scaling
- By mid-2027: compute (and therefore power) is the main constraint on AI progress

## Key Takeaways

- Power is the binding constraint on AI scaling, more than capital or chips
- The grid cannot meet projected demand without massive new generation capacity
- Nuclear (especially SMRs) is the most discussed long-term solution
- The 7-year grid connection wait creates urgency for alternative power strategies
- The AI 2027 scenario's power projections are consistent with real infrastructure
  analysis
