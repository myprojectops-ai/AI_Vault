# Compute Economics

The cost structure of AI training and inference — and how it shapes competition.

## Training Costs

- **GPT-4** (2023): ~$100M training cost, ~2x10^25 FLOP
- **Frontier models (2025-2026):** Estimated $500M-$1B+ per training run
- **AI 2027 Agent-1:** 10^28 FLOP (1000x GPT-4) — implied multi-billion dollar cost
- Training costs are rising with model scale but falling per unit of capability
  (algorithmic efficiency)

## The Efficiency Revolution

- **DeepSeek-R1** trained for less than $6M (vs billions for comparable Western models)
- Cost to train GPT-4-class models has been halving roughly every year
- Algorithmic improvements account for ~50% of AI progress (the other 50% is
  compute scaling)
- In AI 2027: the R&D progress multiplier quantifies how AI accelerates algorithmic
  improvements

## Inference Costs

- Inference (running models) is now the dominant cost for deployed AI
- **Price war:** Models getting dramatically cheaper to run
  - Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite: $2/$12 per million tokens
  - Competition driving prices down across all providers
- Agent-1-mini in AI 2027: "10x cheaper than Agent-1" — mirrors real distillation
  trends

## GPU Supply and Demand

- **Nvidia** dominates AI training hardware (H100, H200, B200 series)
- GPU supply has been constrained since 2023; improving but still tight
- **Nvidia's revenue:** Grew from ~$27B (FY2023) to ~$130B+ (FY2026 estimate)
- Export controls create a bifurcated market: US/allies vs China/restricted

## The Compute Advantage

In the AI 2027 scenario, compute share determines strategic power:

| Entity | Compute Share |
|--------|-------------|
| All US companies | ~70% |
| OpenBrain alone | ~20% |
| China (all) | ~10-14% |
| DeepCent (CDZ) | ~5% |

Real-world figures are similar: US ~74%, China ~14%.

## Economies of Scale

- Larger clusters are more efficient (better GPU utilization, data parallelism)
- This advantages centralized actors (hyperscalers, state-backed Chinese efforts)
- Open-source and smaller labs compete on algorithmic efficiency, not compute

## Key Takeaways

- Training costs are rising in absolute terms but falling per unit of capability
- DeepSeek proved that algorithmic efficiency can compensate for less compute
- Inference costs are dropping rapidly due to competition and distillation
- Nvidia remains the critical hardware bottleneck
- Compute distribution roughly mirrors geopolitical power in AI
- The AI 2027 scenario treats compute as the ultimate bottleneck beyond a
  certain capability level
