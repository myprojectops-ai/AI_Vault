# Agent Capabilities in 2026

What AI agents can and can't do right now — the gap between demos and production.

## What Agents Can Do Well

- **Coding:** Multi-file changes, test generation, bug fixing, refactoring across
  large codebases
- **Research:** Scouring the internet, synthesizing documents, producing reports
- **Data analysis:** Processing spreadsheets, generating visualizations, SQL queries
- **Content creation:** Writing, editing, translating, summarizing
- **Workflow automation:** Email triage, scheduling, form filling, CRM updates
- **Code review:** Identifying bugs, suggesting improvements, enforcing style

## What Agents Struggle With

- **Long-horizon novel tasks:** Multi-day projects with ambiguous requirements
- **Tasks requiring taste or judgment:** Design decisions, strategic planning,
  creative direction
- **Physical-world interaction:** Limited by available APIs and interfaces
- **Real-time collaboration:** Working alongside humans in real-time is clunky
- **Error recovery:** When they go wrong, they often spiral rather than self-correct
- **Context limits:** Despite 1M token windows, agents can lose track in very
  large contexts

## The AI 2027 Comparison

The scenario's Agent-1 (early 2026) description maps closely to current reality:

> "It knows more facts than any human, knows practically every programming language,
> and can solve well-specified coding problems extremely quickly. On the other hand,
> Agent-1 is bad at even simple long-horizon tasks, like beating video games it hasn't
> played before."

This is a remarkably accurate description of current agents.

## The Reliability Problem

- Agents are impressive in cherry-picked examples but unreliable in practice
- The AI 2027 scenario: "AI twitter is full of stories about tasks bungled in some
  particularly hilarious way"
- Real world: the "almost right but not quite" problem is the #1 developer frustration
- Best performance costs hundreds of dollars/month — "you get what you pay for"

## Consumer vs. Enterprise

- **Consumer agents:** Order food, manage budgets, schedule tasks — functional but
  limited. People check in to confirm purchases and decisions.
- **Enterprise agents:** More valuable in structured environments with clear goals
  and feedback loops. 40% of enterprise apps expected to include AI agents by
  end of 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- AI agents in 2026 are powerful tools but unreliable autonomous workers
- Best for well-defined, short-horizon, verifiable tasks
- The reliability gap is narrowing fast but remains significant
- The AI 2027 scenario's description of 2025-2026 agents is accurate
- The leap from "useful assistant" to "autonomous employee" is the current frontier
