# The EU AI Act

Europe's landmark AI regulation — the world's most comprehensive AI law.

## Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| August 1, 2024 | AI Act enters into force |
| February 2, 2025 | Prohibited practices and AI literacy obligations apply |
| August 2, 2025 | Governance rules and GPAI model obligations apply |
| August 2, 2025 | AI Office becomes operational |
| **August 2, 2026** | **Full application** — high-risk AI system rules |
| August 2, 2027 | Extended transition for high-risk AI in regulated products |

## Risk-Based Classification

- **Unacceptable risk (banned):** Social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance
  (with exceptions), manipulative AI, emotion recognition in workplaces/schools
- **High risk:** AI in hiring, credit scoring, law enforcement, healthcare diagnostics,
  critical infrastructure — subject to full compliance framework
- **Limited risk:** Chatbots, deepfakes — transparency obligations (must disclose
  AI interaction)
- **Minimal risk:** Spam filters, AI in games — no specific obligations

## GPAI (General Purpose AI) Rules

- Providers of general-purpose AI models must:
  - Provide technical documentation
  - Comply with EU copyright law
  - Publish training data summaries
- **Systemic risk models** (high-capability GPAI) face additional obligations:
  - Model evaluations and adversarial testing
  - Incident tracking and reporting
  - Cybersecurity measures

## Enforcement and Penalties

- **Up to EUR 35M or 7% of global revenue:** Prohibited practices violations
- **Up to EUR 15M or 3% of global revenue:** Other obligation violations
- **Up to EUR 7.5M or 1% of global revenue:** Supplying incorrect information

## Governance Structure

- **European AI Office:** Governs GPAI model rules
- **National competent authorities:** Oversee AI system rules per member state
- **AI Board + Scientific Panel + Advisory Forum:** Steer governance
- Each member state must establish at least one **AI regulatory sandbox** by
  August 2, 2026

## What's Coming (August 2, 2026)

The major compliance deadline — high-risk AI system rules take full effect:
- Conformity assessments required
- Quality management systems must be in place
- Human oversight requirements
- Transparency and documentation obligations
- Post-market monitoring

## Criticism

- **Too slow:** The AI landscape has changed dramatically since drafting began
  in 2021; the law may be outdated before full application
- **Too rigid:** Risk categories may not map well to rapidly evolving capabilities
- **Competitive impact:** European AI companies face compliance costs that
  US/Chinese competitors don't
- **Enforcement gap:** National implementation varies; some countries slower
  than others

## Key Takeaways

- The EU AI Act is the most comprehensive AI regulation globally
- Full application in August 2026 will test whether the framework works in practice
- GPAI rules directly affect frontier labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google all operate
  in the EU)
- The regulatory sandbox requirement could foster responsible innovation
- Whether this model gets adopted by other jurisdictions remains to be seen
