# AI Sector Impacts

How AI is transforming specific industries — from near-full automation to
augmentation of existing workflows.

## Software Development

- **76%+ of developers** use or plan to use AI tools daily (Stack Overflow 2025)
- **80% of developers** use AI coding agents in workflows (2026 data)
- Junior roles contracting; senior roles shifting to "AI team management"
- By late 2026: fully autonomous agents that take a Jira ticket and deliver a
  merged PR expected to be standard
- See [[coding-agents-landscape]] for detailed tool landscape

## Legal

- **Paralegals:** 80% automation risk by 2026
- **Legal researchers:** 65% automation risk by 2027
- Contract review, document analysis, and case law research heavily automated
- Human lawyers still needed for strategy, client relationships, and court appearances
- Major law firms integrating AI as standard practice

## Healthcare

- **Medical transcription:** 99% automated (already)
- **Medical coding:** 40% projected automation in 2025
- AI-assisted diagnostics improving accuracy in radiology, pathology, dermatology
- Drug discovery timelines compressed via AI molecular modeling
- Regulatory and liability concerns slow clinical deployment

## Finance

- Algorithmic trading and risk analysis already AI-heavy
- AI transforming compliance, fraud detection, and customer service
- Financial analysis and report generation increasingly automated
- Wealth management firms using AI for portfolio optimization
- Regulatory uncertainty limits some applications

## Media and Content

- AI-generated content (text, image, video, audio) is mainstream
- In AI 2027: "Gamers get amazing dialogue with lifelike characters in polished
  video games that took only a month to make"
- Journalism, copywriting, and marketing content partially automated
- Debates over copyright, attribution, and authenticity intensifying

## Customer Service

- Chatbots handling increasing share of customer interactions
- Human agents focused on complex, emotional, or escalated cases
- Cost savings driving rapid adoption across industries

## Key Takeaways

- Software development is the most transformed sector so far
- Legal and healthcare are the next wave of major automation
- Most sectors see augmentation (AI + human) before full automation
- The transition creates demand for AI-skilled workers in every field
- Regulatory and liability concerns slow adoption in healthcare and finance
