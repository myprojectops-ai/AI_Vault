# The AI Investment Boom

The scale of capital flowing into AI is unprecedented in technology history.

## Headline Numbers (2025-2026)

- **Hyperscaler AI spending:** ~$700 billion in datacenter projects in 2026 alone
- **Combined 2025-2026 commitment:** Over $1 trillion from major tech companies
- **Amazon:** $200B projected 2026 spend (up from $131B in 2025)
- **Google:** $175-185B projected 2026 spend (up from $91B in 2025)
- **Global AI capex:** $1T annually (per AI 2027 scenario for 2026)
- **AI contribution to global economy:** Up to $19.9 trillion by 2030

## Stock Market Impact

- AI companies (Nvidia, Microsoft, Google, Meta) drove record market performance
  in 2025
- In AI 2027 scenario: stock market up 30% in 2026, led by OpenBrain and Nvidia
- Real world: Nvidia briefly became the world's most valuable company in 2024-2025
- DeepSeek's January 2025 launch wiped 18% off Nvidia's stock (temporary)

## Startup Activity

- AI wrapper startups received billions in funding
- In AI 2027 scenario: "investors shovel billions into AI wrapper startups,
  desperate to capture a piece of the pie"
- Real world: AI startup funding hit record levels in 2025
- Explosion of B2B SaaS products built on foundation model APIs

## Productivity Impact

- Productivity growth nearly **quadrupled** in AI-exposed industries (7% to 27%,
  2018-2024)
- AI professionals command salaries **56% higher** than peers without AI skills
  (see [[ai-skills-premium]])

## The Sustainability Question

- Revenue doesn't yet match investment at many companies
- OpenBrain in the scenario: $35B revenue against $200B capex in 2026
- Real world: similar questions about ROI for major tech companies
- Bull case: AI is like electricity — front-loaded investment, decades of returns
- Bear case: Overspending on infrastructure before demand materializes

## Key Takeaways

- AI investment is the largest technology buildout in history ($1T+ in 2025-2026)
- Spending is accelerating, not plateauing — 2026 budgets are 50%+ above 2025
- The gap between investment and revenue creates financial risk but also
  competitive moats
- Productivity gains are real and measurable in AI-exposed industries
- The AI 2027 scenario's financial projections are roughly in line with real trends
