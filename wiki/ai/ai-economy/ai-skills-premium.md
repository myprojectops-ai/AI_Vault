# The AI Skills Premium

Workers who can use AI effectively earn significantly more — and the gap is widening.

## The Numbers

- Professionals with specialized AI skills command salaries **up to 56% higher**
  than peers in identical roles without those skills
- Wages are rising **2x faster** in industries most exposed to AI compared to
  least-exposed industries
- AI familiarity is the "most important skill on a resume" (business consultant
  consensus, per AI 2027)

## What Counts as "AI Skills"

- **Tier 1 — AI-native roles:** ML engineering, AI research, prompt engineering,
  AI safety — highest premium
- **Tier 2 — AI-augmented roles:** Developers using AI coding tools, analysts using
  AI for research, designers using generative AI — significant premium
- **Tier 3 — AI-aware roles:** Managers who understand AI capabilities and
  limitations, can direct AI workflows — growing premium

## The Widening Gap

- Workers who adopt AI tools become dramatically more productive
- Those who don't are increasingly compared unfavorably to AI-augmented peers
- Companies hiring for roles prefer candidates who can work alongside AI
- Entry-level workers face the toughest transition: AI can do the tasks that
  traditionally trained juniors

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- "People who know how to manage and quality-control teams of AIs are making
  a killing"
- "Business gurus tell job seekers that familiarity with AI is the most important
  skill to put on a resume"
- By mid-2027: human researchers valued for "research taste" that AI can't replicate

## Key Takeaways

- AI skills are the single largest salary differentiator in the current job market
- The premium exists across industries, not just tech
- The most durable human skills: judgment, taste, client relationships, novel
  problem framing
- The transition to AI-augmented work is happening faster than retraining programs
  can adapt
