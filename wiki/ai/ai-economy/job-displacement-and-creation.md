# AI Job Displacement and Creation

The numbers behind AI's impact on employment — what's actually happening vs.
what's projected.

## Current Job Losses (2025-2026)

- **~55,000 job cuts** directly attributed to AI in 2025 (Challenger, Gray & Christmas),
  out of 1.17 million total layoffs
- **Estimated 200,000-300,000** total US AI-attributable displacement or foregone
  hiring in 2025 (~0.13-0.20% of nonfarm employment)
- The "foregone hiring" effect is larger than direct layoffs — companies simply
  don't fill roles AI can handle

## Projections

- **Goldman Sachs:** 300 million jobs globally exposed to automation by AI
- **WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025:** AI will displace 85 million jobs but create
  97 million new roles by 2030 (net +12 million)
- **Long-term US estimate:** 6-7% of workforce (~11 million workers) displaced
- **McKinsey:** Up to 30% of work hours could be automated by 2030

## Most Affected Sectors

| Sector | Impact | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Legal (paralegals) | 80% automation risk | By 2026 |
| Legal (researchers) | 65% automation risk | By 2027 |
| Medical transcription | 99% automated | Already |
| Medical coding | 40% automated | 2025 |
| Junior software engineering | Severe contraction | 2025-2026 |
| Customer service | Major reduction | Ongoing |
| Data entry / processing | Near-full automation | 2025-2026 |

## New Jobs Created

- AI integration consultants and "AI managers" are in high demand
- Prompt engineering and AI workflow design emerged as roles
- AI safety and alignment research is a growing field
- Companies need people who can manage and quality-control AI teams
- The AI 2027 scenario: "people who know how to manage and quality-control teams
  of AIs are making a killing"

## The Paradox

- Industries most exposed to AI see wages rising **2x faster** than least-exposed
  industries
- AI creates more value for workers who can use it as a tool
- The gap between AI-skilled and non-AI-skilled workers is widening
  (see [[ai-skills-premium]])

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- Late 2026: Junior software engineering in turmoil; 10,000-person anti-AI protest
- July 2027: "For almost every white-collar profession, there are now multiple
  credible startups promising to 'disrupt' it with AI"
- The scenario's economic disruption is more severe than current trends but
  directionally consistent

## Key Takeaways

- Actual AI-driven job losses are currently modest (~0.2% of US employment)
- The bigger effect is foregone hiring, not mass layoffs
- Legal, medical coding, and junior software roles are the earliest casualties
- The WEF projects net job creation, but the transition period is painful
- AI-skilled workers are thriving; the unprepared are falling behind
- The AI 2027 scenario's job disruption timeline is aggressive but not implausible
