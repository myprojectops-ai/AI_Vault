# Chip Export Controls

US semiconductor export restrictions are the primary tool for maintaining America's
AI compute advantage over China.

## Policy Timeline

- **October 2022:** Biden administration's initial export controls on advanced chips
  and chipmaking equipment to China
- **October 2023:** Controls expanded and tightened; closed loopholes
- **January 2025:** Further restrictions on AI chip architecture and cloud access
- **December 2025:** Trump approved conditional Nvidia H200 sales to "approved
  customers" in China, with 25% revenue cut to the US government
- **January 2026:** BIS final rule allowing case-by-case review for exports to
  China/HK/Macau under specific conditions

## What's Restricted

- Advanced AI chips (Nvidia A100, H100, H200, and equivalents)
- Chipmaking equipment (EUV lithography from ASML)
- Advanced chip designs and IP
- Cloud computing access to restricted chips

## Effectiveness

### Working
- China's leading manufacturer (SMIC) stuck at 7nm process; cannot produce
  frontier chips
- US maintains ~74% of global AI-relevant compute
- Forced China to rely on older, less efficient hardware

### Not Working
- Smuggling networks circumvent controls; chips reach China via third countries
- China compensates with algorithmic efficiency (DeepSeek trained R1 for $6M)
- Domestic chip development accelerating (though still ~3 years behind)
- Growing number of models trained entirely on domestic Chinese chips
- **Potential backfire:** If US exports 3M H200 chips in 2026, it would give China
  more AI compute than domestic production could until 2028-29

## Enforcement

- **Operation Gatekeeper** (DOJ, December 2025): Disrupted $160M+ in AI chip
  exports to China/HK
- **BIS Settlement** (January 2026): $1.5M fine for European company's unlawful
  semiconductor transfers
- Enforcement remains a challenge given global supply chain complexity

## Strategic Tensions

- **Hawks:** Maintain or tighten controls; deny China any advanced chips
- **Industry:** Wants to sell to China; argues controls hurt US revenue and
  push China to self-sufficiency faster
- **Pragmatists:** Controls buy time but can't prevent Chinese AI progress
  indefinitely

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- Export controls leave China with ~12% of world AI compute
- China smuggles chips and produces domestic alternatives ~3 years behind
- Controls are necessary but not sufficient; espionage fills the gap
- The scenario treats controls as a delay mechanism, not a solution

## Key Takeaways

- Export controls are the US's primary strategic lever in the AI race
- They've slowed but not stopped Chinese AI progress
- Algorithmic efficiency gains (DeepSeek) undermine the compute-focused strategy
- Enforcement is inconsistent; smuggling and workarounds persist
- The December 2025 H200 decision shows policy is caught between strategic and
  commercial pressures
