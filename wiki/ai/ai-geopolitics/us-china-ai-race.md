# The US-China AI Race

The central geopolitical dynamic shaping AI development: two superpowers competing
for technological supremacy with global consequences.

## The Compute Gap (As of Mid-2025)

| Metric | United States | China |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Share of global AI compute | ~74% | ~14% |
| Leading chip process | 3nm (TSMC) | 7nm (SMIC) |
| Top lab | OpenAI / Anthropic / Google | DeepSeek / Alibaba |
| Estimated frontier model gap | Leading | ~6 months behind |

## US Advantages

- **Compute dominance:** ~74% of global AI-relevant compute
- **Chip manufacturing:** Access to TSMC's cutting-edge nodes via Taiwan
- **Talent concentration:** Top researchers at US-based labs
- **Capital:** Hyperscalers spending $700B+ on AI infrastructure in 2026
- **Ecosystem:** Deep integration between academia, industry, and government

## Chinese Advantages

- **Algorithmic efficiency:** DeepSeek proved frontier performance at fraction
  of the cost (see [[deepseek-and-chinese-open-source]])
- **Centralization:** Ability to consolidate national compute and talent quickly
- **Open-source strategy:** Qwen and DeepSeek building global adoption
- **Domestic chip development:** Training models on domestic chips becoming viable
- **Data:** Massive domestic market and fewer data privacy constraints
- **State backing:** Government can direct resources at scale

## Key Dynamics

### Export Controls (see [[chip-export-controls]])
- US restricts advanced chip sales to China
- Effectiveness debated: China innovates around constraints
- December 2025: Trump approved conditional Nvidia H200 sales to China

### Espionage
- Both sides engage in intelligence gathering on AI capabilities
- In AI 2027: Chinese agencies steal model weights in a coordinated operation
- Real world: DOJ's Operation Gatekeeper disrupted $160M in AI chip exports to China

### Taiwan Factor (see [[taiwan-and-tsmc]])
- TSMC produces 80%+ of advanced AI chips
- Military tensions around Taiwan have direct AI implications
- In AI 2027: both sides consider military action around Taiwan

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

The scenario's central thesis is that race dynamics prevent either side from
prioritizing safety:
- China nationalizes AI research under "DeepCent" collective
- Centralized Development Zone (CDZ) at Tianwan nuclear plant
- Agent-2 weights stolen in February 2027
- By late 2027: US at 70% compute, China at 10%; but China's centralization
  partly compensates
- Neither side can credibly negotiate arms control because trust is absent

## Real-World Divergences from the Scenario

- The scenario may underestimate Chinese algorithmic innovation (DeepSeek)
- The compute gap is real but narrowing in effective terms
- The December 2025 H200 export approval complicates the clean "decoupling" narrative
- Chinese open-source models are gaining global adoption faster than expected
- AI arms control discussions are even less advanced in reality than in the scenario

## Key Takeaways

- The US leads on compute; China competes on efficiency and centralization
- The race dynamic is the single biggest obstacle to coordinated AI safety
- Export controls are a key lever but face enforcement challenges and workarounds
- Taiwan is the strategic chokepoint for the entire AI supply chain
- Neither side has a viable path to decisive, permanent advantage
- The AI 2027 scenario's race dynamics are its most widely-accepted structural argument
