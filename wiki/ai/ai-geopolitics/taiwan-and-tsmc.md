# Taiwan and TSMC

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) is the most strategically important
company in the AI era — and Taiwan is the most dangerous flashpoint.

## Why TSMC Matters

- Produces **80%+ of the world's advanced AI chips**
- Only company capable of manufacturing at 3nm and below
- Nvidia, AMD, Apple, Qualcomm, and all major AI chip designers depend on TSMC
- No viable alternative at scale exists (Intel Foundry and Samsung trail significantly)

## The Strategic Chokepoint

- **If TSMC were disrupted** (by invasion, blockade, or sabotage), global AI
  development would face a severe bottleneck lasting years
- US is building TSMC fabs in Arizona, but these won't match Taiwan capacity for years
- TSMC's most advanced processes remain in Taiwan

## Military Dimension

- China considers Taiwan a breakaway province; has not ruled out force for unification
- In AI 2027: CCP hawks discuss blockade or invasion of Taiwan to neutralize
  the West's chip advantage
- Both sides reposition military assets around Taiwan after the Agent-2 theft
- Real world: military tensions around Taiwan have intensified since 2022

## Diversification Efforts

- **US CHIPS Act:** $52B+ to build domestic semiconductor manufacturing
- **TSMC Arizona:** Fabs under construction, but at older nodes initially
- **European Chips Act:** Similar effort to build European capacity
- **Japan:** TSMC fab in Kumamoto operational; additional capacity planned
- **India:** Emerging as a potential long-term alternative site

## Key Takeaways

- TSMC is the single point of failure for the global AI supply chain
- Taiwan's geopolitical status makes this dependency a security risk
- Diversification is underway but won't match Taiwan's capacity for many years
- Any military conflict involving Taiwan would have immediate, severe AI implications
- The AI 2027 scenario correctly identifies Taiwan as the key strategic leverage point
