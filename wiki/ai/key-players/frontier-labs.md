# Frontier AI Labs

The companies leading the race to build the most capable AI systems.

## OpenAI

- **Founded:** 2015 (as nonprofit); restructured to capped-profit in 2019
- **CEO:** Sam Altman
- **Key models:** GPT-5.x series, o-series reasoning models
- **Strategy:** Aggressive scaling, broad deployment, moving toward open-source
  (partly in response to DeepSeek)
- **Funding:** $13B+ from Microsoft; valued at $150B+ (2025)
- **Notable:** Predicted AGI within 5 years (Altman); setting sights on
  "superintelligence in the true sense of the word"
- **In AI 2027:** The primary inspiration for "OpenBrain"
- **Controversies:** Safety team departures (2024), whistleblower concerns,
  governance restructuring

## Anthropic

- **Founded:** 2021 by former OpenAI researchers (Dario and Daniela Amodei)
- **Key models:** Claude 4.6 family (Opus, Sonnet, Haiku)
- **Strategy:** "Safety-first" positioning; pioneered Constitutional AI and
  Responsible Scaling Policy
- **Differentiator:** 1M token context window; leading interpretability research
  (Microscope)
- **Funding:** $8B+ (Amazon, Google, others)
- **Notable:** Published most interpretability research of any lab; alignment
  faking experiment was landmark

## Google DeepMind

- **Formed:** 2023 merger of Google Brain and DeepMind
- **CEO:** Demis Hassabis (Nobel Prize, 2024)
- **Key models:** Gemini 3.x series
- **Strategy:** Multimodal AI, integration with Google products, scientific AI
  (AlphaFold)
- **Differentiator:** Massive compute access, search data, Android/Chrome
  distribution
- **Predicted AGI within 5 years** (Hassabis)

## Meta AI

- **Head:** Yann LeCun (Turing Award)
- **Key models:** Llama 4 (open-source/open-weights)
- **Strategy:** Open-source as competitive strategy; agentic capabilities
- **Differentiator:** Open-weights models that anyone can deploy; massive social
  media data
- **Impact:** Llama models raised the floor for open-source AI, forcing
  competitors to add more value

## xAI

- **Founded:** 2023 by Elon Musk
- **Key models:** Grok series
- **Strategy:** Integration with X (Twitter); building massive compute cluster
  ("Colossus")
- **Notable:** Rapid scaling of infrastructure; political connections

## The Competitive Dynamic

- All labs are on 3-6 month release cycles
- The gap between them is narrower than ever (see [[model-convergence]])
- Safety vs speed tradeoffs vary: Anthropic most cautious, OpenAI most aggressive
- In AI 2027: "the others to be 3-9 months behind OpenBrain" — this may
  overestimate the lead any single lab holds

## Key Takeaways

- Five US labs dominate frontier AI development
- Their combined spending exceeds $1 trillion in 2025-2026
- Competitive pressure prevents any single lab from prioritizing safety unilaterally
- The "safety-first" lab (Anthropic) must stay competitive to remain relevant
- The AI 2027 scenario's "OpenBrain" is a composite of these labs' capabilities
  and dynamics
