# Chinese AI Labs

China's AI ecosystem is broader and more capable than many Western observers realize.

## DeepSeek

- **Founded:** 2023; backed by quant fund High-Flyer
- **Key models:** DeepSeek-R1, DeepSeek-V3
- **Breakthrough:** R1 matched OpenAI o1 at reasoning for <$6M training cost
- **Strategy:** Full open-source (MIT License); publish training techniques
- **Impact:** Most disruptive AI release of 2025; forced Western labs to reconsider
  their approach
- See [[deepseek-and-chinese-open-source]] for full coverage

## Alibaba (Qwen)

- **Key models:** Qwen series
- **Milestone:** 700M+ Hugging Face downloads by January 2026 — world's most
  widely used open-source AI
- **Strategy:** Open-source ecosystem building; integration with Alibaba Cloud
- **Strength:** Enterprise AI applications, Chinese language capabilities

## Zhipu AI

- **Key models:** GLM series (GLM-4, GLM-Image)
- **Notable:** GLM-Image trained entirely on domestic Chinese chips (early 2026)
- **Strategy:** Full-stack AI company; both research and deployment
- **Backing:** Tsinghua University spinoff; strong government connections

## Baidu

- **Key models:** Ernie series
- **Strategy:** Integration with Baidu's search and cloud platforms
- **Role:** Largest Chinese tech company actively developing foundation models
- **Strength:** Self-driving (Apollo platform), enterprise AI

## China Telecom

- **Key models:** TeleChat3
- **Notable:** Also trained entirely on domestic chips (early 2026)
- **Significance:** State-owned enterprise entering AI signals government push
  for domestic AI independence

## The Ecosystem Dynamic

- Chinese AI models reached ~15% global market share by November 2025
  (up from ~1% a year earlier)
- Open-source strategy is building global adoption, especially in Global South
- Multiple models now trained entirely on domestic chips — reducing vulnerability
  to export controls (see [[chip-export-controls]])
- Government consolidation is accelerating (reflected in AI 2027's CDZ concept)

## In the AI 2027 Scenario

- "DeepCent" represents a government-backed consolidation of Chinese AI
- CDZ at Tianwan nuclear plant concentrates compute
- 50%+ of compute under centralized control
- 6 months behind the leading US lab
- Steals Agent-2 weights in a coordinated intelligence operation

The scenario may underestimate Chinese labs' ability to innovate independently
(DeepSeek's efficiency gains were a surprise to the authors' framework).

## Key Takeaways

- China's AI ecosystem is competitive and rapidly growing
- Open-source strategy is winning global adoption
- Domestic chip training is becoming viable, weakening export control leverage
- Government consolidation is real and accelerating
- The 6-month gap with US labs may narrow faster than expected due to
  algorithmic innovation
