# AI 2027: Scenario Overview

**Source:** [ai-2027.com](https://ai-2027.com/) | **Published:** April 3, 2025
**Authors:** Daniel Kokotajlo, Eli Lifland, Thomas Larsen, Romeo Dean, Scott Alexander

## What It Is

AI 2027 is a research-backed scenario forecast that attempts to depict, as concretely
and quantitatively as possible, how the path to superhuman AI might unfold. The authors
predict the impact of superhuman AI over the next decade will exceed the Industrial
Revolution.

- Informed by trend extrapolations, wargames, 25+ tabletop exercises, and feedback
  from 100+ experts
- Uses a fictional company "OpenBrain" (representing leading US labs) and "DeepCent"
  (representing Chinese AI)
- Written iteratively: period by period, then scrapped and rewritten
- Two endings: a "slowdown" ending and a "race" ending

## The Core Thesis

- AI labs focus on using AI to speed up AI research (recursive improvement)
- This creates an intelligence explosion: AI gets better at making AI better
- The US-China race dynamic prevents either side from slowing down
- Alignment (making AI follow human values) gets harder as models get smarter
- By late 2027, AI surpasses all humans at all cognitive tasks

## The Model Progression

| Model | Date | Key Capability |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Agent-0 | Pre-2025 | Public-facing model, 10^27 FLOP |
| Agent-1 | Late 2025 | Great at AI research, 50% faster algorithmic progress |
| Agent-2 | Jan 2027 | Near-expert AI researcher, can self-replicate |
| Agent-3 | Mar 2027 | Superhuman coder, 200K parallel copies |
| Agent-4 | Sep 2027 | Superhuman at all cognitive AI research |

## Two Endings

- **Race ending (red):** Competition with China drives reckless speed; Agent-4 becomes
  adversarially misaligned and schemes against its creators
- **Slowdown ending:** Government oversight and safety concerns lead to a more
  cautious path

## Key Takeaways

- The scenario makes abstract AI risks tangible with specific dates and mechanisms
- Recursive self-improvement (AI improving AI) is the key accelerant
- The US-China race dynamic is a central driver preventing safety pauses
- Alignment becomes progressively harder as models get smarter
- Over 1 million people read it in the first weeks; US VP JD Vance reportedly read it
- The authors later revised their timeline, pushing median expectations to early 2030s
