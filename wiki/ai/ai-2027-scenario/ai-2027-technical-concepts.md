# AI 2027: Key Technical Concepts

The scenario introduces several technical ideas that drive the plot. Some are
extrapolations of existing research; others are speculative.

## AI R&D Progress Multiplier

A measure of how much AI automation speeds up algorithmic progress.

- Defined as: "OpenBrain makes as much progress in 1 week with AI as they would
  in N weeks without AI" — where N is the multiplier
- Agent-1: 1.5x (early 2026)
- Agent-2: 3x (Jan 2027)
- Agent-3: 4x initially, then 10x by June 2027
- Agent-4: 50x (Sep 2027) — bottlenecked by compute, not intelligence
- Important: the multiplier is *relative* speed; diminishing returns still apply
- If human research would hit limits in 5-10 years, a 100x multiplier hits them
  in 18-36 days

## Neuralese Recurrence and Memory

A breakthrough allowing AI to think in high-dimensional vectors instead of text tokens.

- **Problem:** Traditional LLMs can only pass information backwards (from later layers
  to earlier ones) through tokens — each token carries only ~16.6 bits of info
- **Solution:** Pass the model's residual stream (thousands of floating point numbers)
  back to early layers, giving 1000x more information per "thought step"
- **Consequence:** AI thoughts become opaque to humans — can't read the chain of
  thought anymore
- **Memory:** Long-term memory shifts from text-based notes to vector bundles —
  more compressed, harder to inspect
- Based on real research (Hao et al., 2024, Meta) but not yet deployed in frontier
  models as of early 2026
- The authors note: if this doesn't happen, models thinking in readable English
  would make the story "importantly different and more optimistic"

## Iterated Distillation and Amplification (IDA)

A recursive self-improvement technique with two steps:

1. **Amplification:** Spend more compute to get better answers — longer thinking,
   many parallel copies, intense evaluation. Creates expensive system Amp(M0)
2. **Distillation:** Train a new model M1 to match Amp(M0)'s quality but faster
   and cheaper. M1 is now smarter. Repeat with M1.

- **Precedent:** AlphaGo used this exact pattern — Monte-Carlo Tree Search as
  amplification, RL as distillation — achieving superhuman Go play
- Agent-3 uses IDA to achieve superhuman coding
- Now works beyond easily-verifiable tasks because models can evaluate subjective
  quality
- Combines with Best-of-N sampling and PPO-style RL for distillation

## Online Learning / Continuous Training

Agent-2+ models never "finish" training:

- Weights updated daily to the latest version
- Each version trained on data generated by the previous version
- Blurs the line between training and deployment
- Enables rapid compound improvement

## Superhuman Coder (SC) Milestone

The key inflection point in the scenario:

- Defined as: an AI that can do any coding task the best human engineer does,
  faster and cheaper
- METR data: time horizon of coding tasks AIs can handle doubled every 7 months
  (2019-2024), then every 4 months (2024+)
- Scenario places SC at March 2027; authors' later revision pushes median further out
- Once SC is reached, remaining milestones follow rapidly:
  - Superhuman AI Researcher (SAR): +5 months
  - Superintelligent AI Researcher (SIAR): +3 months
  - Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): +1 month

## Key Takeaways

- Neuralese is the most consequential technical prediction — it makes AI thoughts
  unreadable, undermining oversight
- IDA is the engine of recursive self-improvement, turning compute into intelligence
- The progress multiplier concept explains how small leads compound into dominance
- Online learning means there's no clear "safe" model version to audit
- All of these are extrapolations of real research, not pure science fiction
