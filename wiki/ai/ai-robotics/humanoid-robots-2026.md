# Humanoid Robots in 2026

Physical AI hit a tipping point in 2025 — foundation models matured, hardware
costs dropped, and multiple companies reached second or third-generation platforms.

## Market Size and Growth

- Global embodied AI market: **$4.2 billion** in 2025, growing at **39% annually**
- Humanoid robot shipments (industrial):
  - 2025: 5,000-7,000 units (Deloitte)
  - 2026: 15,000 units projected (Deloitte)
  - 2026: 50,000-100,000 units (Goldman Sachs, more optimistic)
- Material cost per unit: ~$35,000 (2025), projected $13,000-17,000 within a decade

## Current Capabilities

- **What they do:** Move totes/bins, unload containers, handle repetitive
  intralogistics tasks
- **Where they work:** Factories, warehouses, structured environments
- **Autonomy level:** Semi-autonomous; typically human-supervised
- 1X NEO: marketed as "world's first consumer-ready humanoid robot for the home"

## Key Bottlenecks (see [[physical-ai-challenges]])

- Battery life: 90-120 minutes vs 8-20 hours needed for many tasks
- Sim-to-real transfer: Behaviors learned in simulation don't always work physically
- Data scarcity: Much less training data available for physical tasks than text/code
- Hardware reliability: Mechanical systems fail more than software

## Connection to AI 2027 Scenario

The scenario notably does **not** emphasize robotics as a near-term factor.
The AI 2027 forecast focuses on software/cognitive capabilities because:
- Physical AI is compute-constrained in different ways
- The intelligence explosion runs on code, not actuators
- Robotics capabilities listed as one of the slower-advancing areas

This seems roughly correct: humanoid robots are advancing but remain far from
the rapid capability gains seen in software AI.

## Key Takeaways

- 2025-2026 is the inflection point for humanoid robotics (from demos to deployments)
- Market is small but growing rapidly (39% annual growth)
- Consumer-ready robots are arriving but limited in capability
- Physical constraints (battery, reliability) matter more than cognitive ones
- Robotics is behind software AI by several years in terms of practical impact
