# AI Models Timeline: 2025-2026

A tracker of major frontier model releases across all leading labs.

## OpenAI (GPT Series)

- **GPT-5** launched in 2025 with dynamic "thinking" mode to reduce hallucinations
  and significant coding improvements
- **GPT-5.2** (August 2025 cutoff) — iterative improvement
- **GPT-5.4** rolled out March 2026 — latest public model
- Release cadence: roughly every 3-6 months
- OpenAI pivoted toward open-source in mid-2025, partly in response to DeepSeek's
  disruption (see [[deepseek-and-chinese-open-source]])

## Anthropic (Claude Series)

- **Claude 4.5** family released in 2025
- **Claude 4.6** family (early 2026):
  - **Opus 4.6:** Flagship model, 1M token context window (beta)
  - **Sonnet 4.6:** Balanced everyday model, also 1M context
  - **Haiku 4.5:** Fast, cheap option
- Anthropic's strategy: prioritize depth over breadth
- Extended thinking / chain-of-thought reasoning as a core feature

## Google DeepMind (Gemini Series)

- **Gemini 3.0** released late 2025 / early 2026
- **Gemini 3 Deep Think:** Specialized reasoning mode, 84.6% on ARC-AGI-2
- **Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite:** Optimized for speed and cost ($2/$12 per million tokens)
- **Gemini 3.1 Pro:** Frontier-competitive at moderate pricing
- Focus areas: multimodality, reasoning, and developer accessibility

## Meta (Llama Series)

- **Llama 4** released late 2025 / early 2026
- Central focus: **agentic capability** — plans, executes tasks, maintains context
  across extended workflows
- Open-source / open-weights: deploy on own infrastructure with no vendor dependency
- Pushed the industry toward more open releases

## DeepSeek (China)

- **DeepSeek-R1** (January 2025): 671B parameter open-source reasoning model
- Surpassed ChatGPT as most-downloaded iOS app in US
- Triggered 18% drop in Nvidia share price
- See full article: [[deepseek-and-chinese-open-source]]

## Key Trend: Convergence

The story of early 2026 isn't any single model — it's the **shrinking gap** between
frontier models. GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Claude 4.6 are all extraordinary by
historical standards, and differentiation is increasingly about specialization and
pricing rather than raw capability.

See also: [[model-convergence]]

## Key Takeaways

- All major labs are on 3-6 month release cycles; pace is accelerating
- Context windows have expanded massively (1M tokens now available)
- Open-source models (Llama 4, DeepSeek) are competitive with closed models
- Agentic capability is the new frontier across all labs
- The gap between #1 and #5 is smaller than ever
