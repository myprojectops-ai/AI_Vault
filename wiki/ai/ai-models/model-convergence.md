# Model Convergence: The Shrinking Frontier Gap

The gap between the best and fifth-best AI model is smaller than ever. What does
this mean for competition, safety, and the industry?

## The Pattern

- In 2023, GPT-4 had a clear lead over all competitors
- By early 2025, Claude 3.5, Gemini 1.5, and Llama 3 were competitive
- By early 2026, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude 4.6, Llama 4, and DeepSeek models
  are all "extraordinary by any historical standard"
- Differentiation is increasingly about **specialization and pricing** rather than
  raw capability

## Why It's Happening

- **Algorithmic knowledge diffuses:** Research papers, open-source releases, and
  employee mobility spread techniques quickly
- **Scaling laws are shared knowledge:** Everyone knows how to scale transformers;
  the recipe is public
- **Open-source raises the floor:** DeepSeek and Llama force closed labs to compete
  on value-add, not just capability
- **Post-training is the new moat:** The difference is in fine-tuning, RLHF/DPO,
  safety work, and product integration

## Implications

- **For safety:** No single lab can safely slow down — competitors will fill the gap
  (this is the core dynamic in [[ai-2027-overview]])
- **For business:** Raw model capability is commoditizing; value moves to
  applications, data, and integration
- **For open-source:** As the frontier becomes cheaper to reach, open models get
  more competitive
- **For regulation:** Harder to regulate if the technology is widely distributed
  across many capable actors

## Key Takeaways

- The "moat" in AI is shifting from model capability to ecosystem and product
- Convergence makes unilateral safety pauses strategically costly
- Open-source is a permanent competitive force, not a temporary disruption
- The AI 2027 scenario assumes one lab leads by 3-9 months — this may be optimistic
  given convergence trends
