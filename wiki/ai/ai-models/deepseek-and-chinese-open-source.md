# DeepSeek and China's Open-Source AI Surge

How a Chinese lab upended the AI industry with a cheap, open reasoning model —
and what followed.

## The DeepSeek Moment (January 2025)

- **DeepSeek-R1** released January 20, 2025: open-source reasoning model, 671B
  parameters
- Surpassed OpenAI's o1 on reasoning benchmarks
- Developed in just 2 months for less than $6 million
- Released under MIT License (fully permissive)
- Published full training process and techniques
- By January 27, surpassed ChatGPT as the most-downloaded free app on iOS in the US
- Nvidia stock dropped 18% on the news

## Why It Mattered

- Shattered the narrative that frontier AI requires billions of dollars
- Demonstrated that clever algorithmic efficiency can compensate for compute
  disadvantage (see [[chip-export-controls]])
- Forced Western labs to reconsider their moats
- OpenAI pivoted toward open-source partly in response

## Subsequent Releases

- **DeepSeek-V3-0324** (March 2025): MIT License
- **DeepSeek-R1-0528** (May 2025): MIT License
- Consistent pattern: rapid iteration, full openness

## The Broader Chinese Open-Source Ecosystem

- Chinese AI models reached ~15% global market share by November 2025 (up from ~1%
  a year earlier)
- **Alibaba Qwen:** 700M+ downloads on Hugging Face by January 2026 — world's most
  widely used open-source AI system
- **Zhipu GLM-Image** and **China Telecom TeleChat3:** Trained entirely on domestic
  chips (early 2026)
- Open-source as strategy: builds ecosystem, reduces dependency on Western APIs,
  accelerates adoption in Global South

## Connection to AI 2027 Scenario

The AI 2027 scenario's "DeepCent" mirrors real dynamics:
- Chinese labs doing impressive work with limited compute
- ~6 months behind frontier US models
- Government consolidation of research resources
- The compute gap is real: US holds ~74% of global AI compute vs China's ~14%

But the scenario may underestimate Chinese algorithmic innovation — DeepSeek's
efficiency gains were a surprise even to Western labs.

## Key Takeaways

- DeepSeek proved frontier-quality AI doesn't require frontier-level budgets
- Open-source is now a viable competitive strategy, not just an ideological choice
- China's AI ecosystem is broader than DeepSeek — Qwen, GLM, and others are thriving
- The open-source wave is forcing the entire industry toward more transparency
- Training on domestic chips is becoming viable, potentially undermining export controls
